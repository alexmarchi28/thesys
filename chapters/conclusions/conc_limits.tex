\section{Limitations of the Work}

OMNeT++, SUMO, PLEXE, and Simu5G enable a useful and coherent study, but they do not fully describe the complexity of the real world. As a consequence, the results should be read primarily as a functional validation of the system, not as a direct prediction of on-road performance. This is especially true for the comparison between LTE and 5G, which also depends on the adopted radio models and handover parameters.

A second limitation is the homogeneity of the scenario. The vehicles in the platoon are all identical in terms of dynamic model, controller, and communication equipment, whereas in reality it is reasonable to expect differences among vehicles, sensors, antennas, and actuation times. This too can influence both platoon stability and the effectiveness of the \emph{fallback}.

Finally, the communication channel was modeled selectively. The realistic scenario includes interference and handovers, but does not fully consider all possible sources of degradation, such as fading and shadowing, loss of line of sight, external radio traffic, or varying environmental conditions. For this reason, the thesis does not provide a definitive scenario, but rather a coherent basis for subsequent and more realistic analyses.
