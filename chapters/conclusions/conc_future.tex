\section{Future Developments}

The most natural future development is the construction of a more realistic scenario, with heterogeneous vehicles, mixed traffic, more complex road geometries, obstacles, and richer propagation models. An extension of this kind would make it possible to better understand how much of the observed robustness depends on the \emph{SafeSwitch} mechanism and how much instead depends on simplifications in the scenario.

A second development concerns a more controlled comparison between LTE and 5G, keeping the scenario, radio parameters, and replication settings fixed. In this way it would be possible to isolate the effect of technological migration more clearly and also measure additional metrics, such as latency, jitter, and handover duration.

In this context, the proposed 5G implementation therefore represents a solid and reusable basis for future experimental work, enabling the study of scenarios based on the integration of heterogeneous networks such as 802.11p, VLC, and 5G.
