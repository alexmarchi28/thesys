\section{Thesis Structure}

The rest of the thesis is organized as follows. Chapter~2 presents the state of the art on platooning, longitudinal controllers, the V2X technologies considered, and multi-channel approaches, concluding with a discussion of the \emph{SafeSwitch} mechanism that serves as the direct reference for the experimental work. Chapter~3 then describes the development and simulation environment, clarifying the role of OMNeT++, Veins, PLEXE, SUMO, and Simu5G in the adopted toolchain.

Chapter~4 addresses the import of the logic from the reference project, focusing on scenarios, metrics, PDR integration, the \emph{SafeSwitch} mechanism, and the migration of the cellular component from SimuLTE to Simu5G. Chapter~5 presents and discusses the results obtained, including both the comparison with a \emph{naive} fallback and the analysis of the realistic 5G scenario. Finally, Chapter~6 summarizes the main contributions of the thesis, discusses its limitations, and outlines possible future developments.

Appendix~\ref{ch:setup} details the preparation of the experimental environment, including workspace organization, build steps, and operational validation of the toolchain.
