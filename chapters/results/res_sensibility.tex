\section{Sensitivity to Channel Assumptions}

To evaluate how much the results of the realistic scenario depend on the underlying physical model, a brief sensitivity analysis was carried out on the two main components of the radio channel: \emph{shadowing} and \emph{fading}. Four cases were considered: both components active, only \emph{shadowing}, only \emph{fading}, and neither of them active. The goal is not to estimate a better parameter set, but to understand which phenomena remain stable as the propagation model varies and which, instead, change substantially.

The first element that remains stable in all tests is the behavior of IEEE~802.11p. Loss bursts always appear in the same time windows, corresponding to the interference zones already discussed in the realistic scenario. This confirms that, for the 802.11p channel, scenario geometry and interfering traffic remain the main cause of the observed degradation; the propagation model mainly changes its intensity and dispersion across the different vehicles.

The sensitivity of the cellular component is much more marked. In the FER of the predecessor frames, the case without \emph{shadowing} and \emph{fading} shows almost only isolated spikes, whereas as soon as a stochastic component is introduced the behavior changes drastically. With only \emph{shadowing}, long intervals of high FER appear on the C-V2X channel, and with \emph{shadowing}+\emph{fading} the degradation becomes even more irregular and persistent. The case with only \emph{fading} is also significantly worse than the baseline, but with a more gradual increase in the second part of the simulation. In the new plots, therefore, the cellular network no longer appears merely as a source of short transients: depending on the channel model, it can become one of the main sources of loss.

VLC is in turn very sensitive, but with different behavior between predecessor FER and leader FER. In the mosaic dedicated to predecessor frames, VLC degradation grows especially in the cases with \emph{fading}, up to long stretches with FER close to \(1\), whereas in the deterministic case it remains almost negligible. In the mosaic of leader frames the effect is even clearer: in all three stochastic cases, VLC tends to saturate, with much earlier transitions when \emph{shadowing} is present. This is consistent with the fact that leader information traverses a longer communication chain and therefore accumulates more opportunities for loss than predecessor information.

These results lead to a different conclusion than the previous version of this section. Quantitative differences across runs, and also between the present thesis and the reference paper, do not depend only on the LTE\(\rightarrow\)5G migration, but also substantially on the adopted channel assumptions. In particular, the new tests show that the cellular component and VLC can move from a secondary role to a dominant role in FER simply by changing \emph{shadowing} and \emph{fading}. As a consequence, the results of the realistic scenario should be interpreted above all as evidence of the system's functional consistency, whereas any strict numerical comparison would require a much more rigorous calibration of the radio model.
