\chapter{Results Analysis}
\label{ch:results}

This chapter evaluates the behavior of the reimplementation described in Chapter~\ref{ch:safeswitch_5g}, with two complementary objectives. On the one hand, it aims to verify that the logic of \emph{SafeSwitch} reproduces the expected qualitative behavior in the most significant artificial scenarios; on the other hand, it seeks to understand to what extent the transition from SimuLTE to Simu5G changes the results in the more realistic scenario, where interference, handovers, and channel models interact with platoon dynamics.

The analysis is organized into two steps. It first considers the comparison with a \emph{naive} fallback, in order to highlight the role of the \emph{gap control} phase. It then moves to the realistic scenario with interference on IEEE~802.11p and cellular handovers, comparing the obtained results with those reported in the reference paper \cite{segata2023multi-technology}. The chapter closes with a critical discussion of the limits of quantitative interpretation.

In the plots, the symbols \(\times\) and \(+\) indicate \emph{failure} and \emph{recovery} events, respectively, while the gray bands highlight the cellular handover intervals. When link quality is analyzed, the \emph{Frame Error Rate} (FER) is used, defined as
\[
\mathrm{FER} = \frac{N_{\mathrm{expected}} - N_{\mathrm{received}}}{N_{\mathrm{expected}}},
\]
which, for the same observation window, is complementary to the PDR used internally by the \emph{SafeSwitch} monitor.
