\chapter{Methodology and Tools}
\label{env}

After framing the problem and the state of the art, this chapter describes the development and simulation environment adopted for the thesis work, clarifying the role of the main software tools and the reasons for combining them. The focus is on the concrete architecture of the toolchain, with particular attention to the integration between discrete-event simulation, network simulation, and traffic simulation.

The validity of the experiments depends on the possibility of jointly representing phenomena belonging to different domains: vehicle dynamics, longitudinal control, road mobility, and wireless communications. The environment adopted in this thesis was therefore selected to support a coupled and modular simulation, in line with the consolidated literature on vehicular simulation and cooperative driving \cite{varga2008overview,sommer2011bidirectionally,segata2014plexe,nardini2020simu5g}.
