\section{Multi-Technology Architecture}
\label{sec:s5g_architecture}

In the adopted multi-technology architecture, each vehicle in the platoon simultaneously has three communication interfaces, namely 802.11p, VLC, and a cellular component, and beacons are transmitted in parallel over the active technologies. The follower then uses the information received from the leader and the predecessor together with local sensors to regulate its own dynamics.

The key point is that the interfaces are not treated as mere passive redundancy. The number of technologies considered reliable determines which operational state of the platoon can be maintained, and therefore which controller and which gap are admissible at that moment. The logic of \emph{SafeSwitch} is built precisely on this relationship between connectivity, control, and inter-vehicle distance.
